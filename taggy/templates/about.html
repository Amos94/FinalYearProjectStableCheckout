Tags


When tagging a sentence, identify the main focus of the sentence. You can select one or several tags. There are 11 tags, described below:

  • PERS represents any personal information provided or requested by the post author. Personal information is defined as anything not clinical. For instance:
    • "I am 32 years old" [This would be tagged as PERS/provide]
    • "Where you born in the States?" [This would be tagged as PERS/request]
    • "I love Twinkies!" [This would be tagged as PERS/provide]
    • "My sister lives nearby" [This would be tagged as PERS/provide]

  • FIND represents any health finding, sign, symptom or side effect, related to cancer or any other health condition. For instance:
    • "My fingers are swollen and hurt" [This would be tagged as FIND/provide]
    • "Did anyone else get nausea?" [This would be tagged as FIND/request]
    • "She lost weight on that treatment" [This would be tagged as FIND/provide]
    • "I feel dizzy" [This would be tagged as FIND/provide]
    • "If the headaches get too severe, you can always take some Tylenol" [This would be tagged as FIND/provide]

  • TEST represents any discussion of diagnostic procedure, such as mammograms or genetic testing. Select this tag when the sentence is about the procedure itself, rather than the results of the procedure. For instance:
    • "Should I get a genetic test if my mom had breast cancer too?" [This would be tagged as TEST/request]
    • "What are the risks of the biopsy?" [This would be tagged as TEST/request]
    • "I remember having to do a mammogram after breast reconstruction" [This would be tagged as TEST/provide]

  • DIAG represents any discussion of diagnoses, measurements, and results of tests related to cancer or any other health condition. This tag is different from tag FIND, because DIAG relates to actual clinical diagnoses, whereas FIND relates to symptoms. DIAG is also different from TEST, because it relates to the results of a test rather than the test itself. For instance:
    • "My blood pressure is good (120/80)" [This would be tagged as DIAG/provide]
    • "The tumor size is 2cm" [This would be tagged as DIAG/provide]
    • "Does anyone have arthritis?" [This would be tagged as DIAG/request]
    • "The nurse said I probably have edema" [This would be tagged as DIAG/provide]

  • TREA represents any discussion of treatments, including procedures, medications and therapeutic devices. For instance:
    • "Do you take any steroid?" [This would be tagged as TREA/request]
    • "I am on AC" [This would be tagged as TREA/provide]
    • "I had a bilateral mastectomy" [This would be tagged as TREA/provide]
    • "Where should I install my port-a-cath?" [This would be tagged as TREA/request]

  • HSYS represents any discussion of health systems and institutions, including, doctors, practices, nurses, hospitals, and insurance companies. For instance:
    • "I am not in love with my onc" [This would be tagged as HSYS/provide]
    • "Cigna doesn't reimburse the bra!" [This would be tagged as HSYS/provide]
    • "What do you think of the new health reforms?" [This would be tagged as HSYS/request]

  • DAIL represents any discussion related to daily cancer-related experience. For instance:
    • "Install your port on the right, this way your car seat belt won't bug you" [This would be tagged as DAIL/provide]
    • "Which shampoo do you use?" [This would be tagged as DAIL/request]
    • "Heat pads helped me a great deal" [This would be tagged as DAIL/provide]

  • NUTR represents any discussion related to nutrition. For instance:
    • "Ginger helps for vomiting" [This would be tagged as NUTR/provide]
    • "I cant digest fish oil" [This would be tagged as NUTR/provide]
    • "What is a healthy sugar substitute?" [This would be tagged as NUTR/request]

  • ALTR represents any discussion related to alternative and integrative medicine. For instance:
    • "Acupuncture is helpful" [This would be tagged as ALTR/provide]
    • "Did massages help with lymphedema?" [This would be tagged as ALTR/request]
    • "Rosemary oil is supposed to help with memory" [This would be tagged as ALTR/provide]

  • RSRC represents any discussion or pointer to another post or extrenal resource. For instance:
    • "The following article was fascinating" [This would be tagged as RSRC/provide]
    • "Anybody seen any study on the subject?" [This would be tagged as RSRC/request]
    • "There was a long thread on that question" [This would be tagged as RSRC/provide]

  • MISC represents any other miscalleneous topic of discussion, or any sentence which on its own does not provide any information. For instance:
    • "Feel better!" [This would be tagged as MISC/provide]
    • "I am new so I probably posted this in the wrong board!" [This would be tagged as MISC/provide]
    • "Can someone explain this?" [This would be tagged as MISC/request]
    • "Ha!" [This would be tagged as MISC/provide]
